Ecstasy

Ecstasy (he/they) is a transmasc tiefling with pitch black eyes, a trait shared amongst most Cepheus family members. He was the older of his identical twin sister, one twin is born with a sun birthmark (him), the other with a moon birthmark (his sister).

The Cepheus family is cursed to always and only have 2 twins, each marked by their respective celestial body. The sun child is destined to inherit the power and fortune of the family, and for the past hundred generations, this has been the case.

X was born with the sun birthmark, but never wanted to become the head of the family, so he ran away before his 18th birthday, met a hag, and traded his valuable name for fame and power.

He took on his stage name, Ecstasy, and went on to become one of the most popular artists of his time, known throughout the planescape for his punk rock music.

Nokia

Nokia (any/all) is an aarakocra (bird-person) bard/artificer that specializes in alchemy and acid/poison based magic. He's a mischevious grafiti artist and often uses his ability of flight to put art in hard to reach/clean places.

He's a college of creation bard, with his signature paint-stained waistpiece, which is a stolen noble's silk. He also sports a celestial patterned cloth over his left shoulder to honor a fallen party member.

Due to extensive exposure to loud noises/explosions, he develops hearing loss that eventually becomes full deafness. He later learns sign langauge and utilizes telepathy to communicate with others.

Rithil

Rithil (he/it) is a rakshasa (reflavored kalashtar) wizard/cleric who discovers the lost art of dunamancy, time and space magic. While he has a level in arcane cleric, most of his abilities are connected to his wizard class.

He used to be a powerful fiend in hell, like other rakshasas, before he met his future wife, an adventurer. He chose to give up his fiendish powers to live a life with her on the material plane.

This was all well and good for many years, as they worked as magic item merchants after her adventuring ended, up until she died from a cursed artifact. Terrified of losing her, Rithil promises to find a way to bring her back, to which her last words were "Don't, I will wait for you."

Overcome with grief, he studies dunamancy to try and turn back time to bring her back from the dead, but it may not work...

Rithil's species, rakshasa, are man-eating monsterous fiends from The Nine Hells. He did use to be one of these monsters, even still having the backwards wrists, a trademark of their species.

Spoilers for Rithil's arc

Rithil's attempt to resurrect his wife fails, as her soul is not willing to return. This breaks him and sends him over the edge, he spirals and regresses back into his evil ways

He eventually becomes the BBEG of a campaign of mine, where he kills the goddess of magic to release spellcasting upon the material plane, as the lack of magic users when his wife died led to not being able to save her.
