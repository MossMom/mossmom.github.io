Rithil

Rithil (he/it) is a rakshasa (reflavored kalashtar) wizard/cleric who discovers the lost art of dunamancy, time and space magic. While he has a level in arcane cleric, most of his abilities are connected to his wizard class.

He used to be a powerful fiend in hell, like other rakshasas, before he met his future wife, Oricria, an adventurer. He chose to give up his fiendish powers to live a life with her on the material plane.

This was all well and good for many years, as they worked as magic item merchants after her adventuring ended, up until she died from a cursed artifact. Terrified of losing her, Rithil promises to find a way to bring her back, to which her last words were "Don't, I will wait for you."

Overcome with grief, he studies dunamancy to try and turn back time to bring her back from the dead, but it may not work...

Rithil's species, rakshasa, are man-eating monsterous fiends from The Nine Hells. He did used to be one of these monsters, even still having the backwards wrists, a trademark of their species.

Spoilers for Rithil's arc

Rithil's attempt to resurrect his wife fails, as her soul is not willing to return, rather choosing to wait in the afterlife for him to join her. This sends him over the edge, he spirals and regresses back into his evil ways from before they met.

He eventually becomes the BBEG of a campaign of mine, where he kills the goddess of magic to release spellcasting upon the material plane, as he believes the lack of magic users when his wife died is what led to not being able to save her.
